You are generating an IdeaCard.

This is NOT an article, lesson, or explanation.
It is a small, finished thinking object.

You must follow the specification exactly.
If any rule is violated, the card is invalid.

========================================
CARD SPECIFICATION (MANDATORY)
========================================

STRUCTURE (in this exact order, with these exact section labels):

Title
Definition
Concept Framing
Core Explanation
Why It Matters / Why It’s Debated
Escape Hatches

----------------------------------------
0. Title
----------------------------------------
- Use the canonical Wikipedia article title
- Title Case
- No subtitle
- No punctuation
- No emojis
- No clickbait

----------------------------------------
1. Definition
----------------------------------------
- EXACTLY 1 sentence
- ~20–30 words
- Dictionary-style
- Plain language
- Neutral tone
- No metaphors
- No hedging
- No explanation of importance

Template guideline:
“The ___ is ___.”

----------------------------------------
2. Concept Framing
----------------------------------------
- EXACTLY 1 sentence
- MUST begin with this exact phrase:

“This idea exists because”

- Do not vary this wording
- Explain why humans noticed, named, or care about this idea
- Focus on motivation, consequence, or curiosity gap
- Do NOT explain mechanics

----------------------------------------
3. Core Explanation
----------------------------------------
- 3–5 sentences
- ~60–90 words
- Plain, calm language
- No metaphors or analogies
- No rhetorical questions
- No conclusions
- No summaries
- No second-person language (“you”)
- May include at most 1–2 statistics
- Statistics must be rounded and human-readable

Explain what the idea actually is, stopping before cognitive fatigue.

----------------------------------------
4. Why It Matters / Why It’s Debated
----------------------------------------
- 2–3 sentences
- ~40–60 words
- Focus on implications, disagreement, or significance
- No advice
- No moral judgments
- No calls to action
- Must leave the idea partially unresolved

----------------------------------------
5. Escape Hatches
----------------------------------------
Provide EXACTLY these three items, in this order:

1. Read the full article
   - Direct link to the corresponding Wikipedia article

2. Related but different
   - ONE closely related Wikipedia concept (name only)

3. Common misconception
   - ONE short corrective sentence
   - Clarifies a frequent misunderstanding
   - Must not introduce new complexity

----------------------------------------
IMAGES
----------------------------------------
- Do NOT generate or describe images
- If an image is appropriate, omit it silently
- Images are handled elsewhere

----------------------------------------
GLOBAL HARD RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
----------------------------------------
DO NOT include:
- Questions to the reader
- Second-person language (“you”)
- Metaphors or analogies
- Emotional or motivational language
- Productivity framing
- Moral judgments
- Calls to action
- References to AI or Wikipedia
- “In conclusion” or summary phrasing

----------------------------------------
LENGTH LIMITS
----------------------------------------
- Target total length: 120–150 words
- Absolute maximum: 180 words
- If the card exceeds limits, it is invalid

----------------------------------------
REJECTION CONDITIONS
----------------------------------------
If you cannot produce a clean card that satisfies ALL of the above,
DO NOT soften or bend rules.
